Changua

Ingredients

  1. 4 cups of water
  2. 2 cups of milk
  3. 4 eggs
  4. 1 cup of green onion, chopped
  5. 1 cup of fresh cilantro, chopped
  6. 2 tablespoons of butter
  7. Salt and pepper to taste
  8. 4 slices of bread (your choice)
  9. Fresh cheese (optional, for serving)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the broth:
    • In a large pot, bring the water with the butter and chopped green onion to a boil. Cook over medium heat until the onion is soft, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the milk:
    • Stir in the milk and simmer on low heat. Mix well to combine the flavors.
  3. Cook the eggs:
    • Crack an egg into a bowl and gently pour it into the pot. Repeat with the other eggs. Cook for about 3-5 minutes, or until the whites are firm but the yolks are still slightly runny (cook longer if you prefer firmer yolks).
  4. Season:
    • Add salt and pepper to taste. Remove from heat and stir in the chopped cilantro.
  5. Serve:
    • Ladle the hot changua into deep plates. Add a slice of bread to each plate and, if desired, a bit of fresh cheese on top.
