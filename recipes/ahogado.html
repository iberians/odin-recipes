Ahogado de carne con plátano (Beef Ahogado with Plantains)

Ingredients

  1. 500 g beef (such as flank or chuck), cut into chunks
  2. 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  3. 1 large onion, chopped
  4. 2 cloves garlic, minced
  5. 1 ripe tomato, chopped
  6. 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  7. 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  8. Salt and pepper to taste
  9. 4 cups beef broth (or water)
  10. 1 bay leaf
  11. cilantro, chopped
  12. 4 ripe plantains (preferably yellow or "guineo" plantains)
  13. Oil

Instructions

  1. Cook the Beef:
    • In a large pot, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until golden.
    • Add the beef pieces and brown on all sides.
    • Incorporate the bell pepper, tomatoes, cumin, paprika, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
    • Pour in the beef broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover the pot, and simmer for about 1.5 hours or until the meat is tender. Check occasionally and add more broth or water if necessary.
  2. Prepare the Plantains:
    • While the beef is cooking, peel the plantains and cut them into approximately 2 cm thick slices.
    • In a frying pan, heat enough oil for frying the plantains. Once hot, add the plantain slices and fry until golden brown on both sides. Remove and place on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  3. Serve:
    • Once the beef is ready, remove the bay leaf and adjust salt and pepper to taste.
    • Serve the beef in deep plates with a little of the broth on top.
    • Accompany with the fried plantain slices on the side.
    • If desired, sprinkle chopped fresh cilantro over the dish before serving.
